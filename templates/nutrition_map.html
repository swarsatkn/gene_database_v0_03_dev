Recommended Daily Allowance (RDA)

1. Iron: Adult Men (17 mg/day); Adult Women (21 mg/day); Pregnancy 35 mg/day; Lactation 21 mg/day

Note: Pair iron-rich foods with vitamin C sources to enhance absorption. Avoid tea or coffee immediately after meals.

2. Vitamin B12: Adult Men (2.4 ug/day); Adult Women (2.4 ug/day); Pregnancy 2.6 ug/day; Lactation 2.8 ug/day

Note: Vegetarians, elderly individuals, and patients with malabsorption may require fortified foods or supplementation under medical guidance

How this map benefits you?

Visualizing geographic nutrition profiles allows medical experts and researchers to quickly correlate regional dietary patterns with conditions such as Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) and anemia. By identifying localized deficiencies or an abundance of Iron and Vitamin B12, healthcare providers can confidently prescribe proactive interventions, suggest geographically tailored dietary plans, and improve the precision of patient screening and therapy.
